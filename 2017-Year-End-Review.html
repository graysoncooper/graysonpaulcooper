My 2017 Year End Review: Success, Failures, and Lessons

At the beginning of this year, I only wanted to focus on the future and plan on how I'd continue to improve. (I wrote about those things here). Until a bit ago I read Tomas Laurinavicius' email about his year in review. In his email, he lists the top 10 successes, failures, lessons, songs, books, etc. It was an interesting email and so I forwarded it to a good friend of mine, Forrest.

After Forrest read it, he texted me and said, “Thanks so much for sending me that email. I read it then thought about all the things that I did in 2017 and I am really happy about who I am today.”

His text inspired me to do it for myself. After going through the exercise of listing these out, it magnified how much I got done in a year (AND how much I can potentially get done in the next). Here are my top 10 successes, failures, books, songs, and lessons from 2017.

In 2017, I suceeded at…

  1. starting work for a company I like with peers I respect.
  2. getting promoted twice and fundamentally changing the sales team at Rev.
  3. raising a wonderful dog, Willow.
  4. improving self awareness.
  5. working for myself for 5 months.
  6. starting a relationship by being geniune, honest & fell in love.
  7. getting in great shape / working out 4 times a week at least.
  8. paying off all my student loan debt (18k).
  9. maxing out Roth IRA contributions.
  10. surrounding myself with more friends that help encourage me to grow as a person.

I failed to...

  1. meditate consistently.
  2. let go of things I cannot control.
  3. focus on the right things.
  4. generate a living by working for myself with Spark School.
  5. launch a personal blog and write monthly.
  6. create & keep a morning routine.
  7. read a lot of books.
  8. visit my sister abroad.
  9. encourage and help my parents to find their health / happiness.
  10. learn spanish.

My favorite books were...

  1. How to Fail at Almost Everything and Still Win Big: Kind of the Story of My Life by Scott Adams<
  2. Predictable Revenue by Aaron Ross
  3. Managing Oneself by Peter Drucker
  4. The Bhagavad Gita by Eknath Easwaran
  5. Ultimate Sales Machine by Chet Holmes

According to Spotify, my most played songs were...

  1. Waves - Kayne West
  2. Remedy - Rendezvous At Two (feat. Just Chris)
  3. Heartlines (Race Banyon Remix) - BROODS
  4. Norf Norf - Vince Staples
  5. Next Year (RAC Remix) - Two Door Cinema Club
  6. Love is Alive - Lous the Child, Elohim
  7. HUMBLE - Kendrick Lamar
  8. Hazelnut Butter - Lafa Taylor, Medium Troy
  9. Swim High - BABYTEETH, Rivka Rose
  10. Do You Go Up - Khai

I I learned a deeper meaning of these lessons..

  1. Wherever you go, there you are.
  2. Optimism is a choice (a fucking hard choice sometimes).
  3. Who you spend time with influences your behaviors and thoughts.
  4. You are a product of your thoughts.
  5. You can pour your heart into something and it still not pan out.
  6. Working smarter is better than working hard, but working hard is a good thing most of the time.
  7. If you believe in yourself and act confident, you're most of the way there.
  8. If you love, do it with all your heart.
  9. Make self development a priority.
  10. Think long term.

I am happier now than I was at the beginning of 2017. This exercise helped remind me how grateful I am to be where I am now. If you're looking for an activity to make you grateful, try this one!
